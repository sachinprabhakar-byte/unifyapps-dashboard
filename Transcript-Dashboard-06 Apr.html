📐 Scoring Methodology

How each call is scored across 4 dimensions (total: 100 pts)
30 pts

1. Tone & Language

Assesses the emotional register of the conversation — positive, collaborative, frustrated, or tense. High scores reflect constructive, warm language from both sides.

25 pts

2. Progress & Momentum

Measures whether the project is moving forward — milestones hit, deliverables agreed, blockers resolved. Stalled progress or repeated items reduce this score.

25 pts

3. Client Engagement

Evaluates how actively the client participates — asking questions, providing timely inputs, attending calls, expressing interest. Low engagement signals churn risk.

20 pts

4. Risk & Issue Density

Deduction-based. Counts unresolved blockers, deadline pressure, escalation signals, and recurring complaints. More issues = lower score.

Score Bands
RangeLabelRisk LevelSignal
80–100🟢 PositiveLow RiskStrong momentum, satisfied client, no blockers
60–79🟡 NeutralMedium RiskModerate progress, some concerns worth monitoring
40–59🔴 NegativeHigh RiskNotable friction, blockers present, engagement declining
0–39🔴🔴 CriticalCriticalEscalation risk, significant dissatisfaction or stall
How account score is calculated: Each call is scored independently across the 4 dimensions. The account's daily score is the average of all calls on that day. The overall score shown in the overview is the average across the most recent day's calls.
